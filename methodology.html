Methodology

A serving of beef is calculated using the Government of Canada’s Food Guide and their recommend serving size of meat and number of servings per day.

We used data from the Environmental Working Group’s report – Meat Eater’s Guide – for the greenhouse emissions for beef consumption, 27 kg CO2e per kg of consumed beef.

The results from using the emissions calculator are computed using calculations from the United States Environmental Protection Agency. We have outlined each equation.

Individual annual emissions (kg CO2e)

= # of times beef eaten per week * # of servings * 75g per serving size / 1000 1 * 52.1429 2 * 27 3

  1. 1) To put beef consumption into kilograms
  2. 2) Number of weeks in the year
  3. 3) 27kg CO2e per kg of consumed beef.

KM driven by an average car

= Individual annual emissions (kg CO2e) / 0.322 kg CO2e * 1.60934 1

  1. 1) To convert from miles to km

Calculation from EPA site:

8.89 × 10-3 metric tons CO2/gallon gasoline × 1/22 miles per gallon × 1 CO2, CH4, and N2O/0.986 CO2 = 4.17 x 10-4 metric tons CO2E /mile

Global Annual Emissions based on individual results (gigatonnes) (2017)

= Individual annual emissions (kg CO2e) * 9,772,000,000 1 / 1,000,000,000,000 2

  1. 1) World population in 2050 6
  2. 2) To convert to gigatonnes

Percentage of Global Greenhouse Threshold (2017)

= Global Annual Emissions / 21 Gt 1

  1. 1) Threshold determined by world leaders to prevent a 2 degree celsius raise in global temperature
